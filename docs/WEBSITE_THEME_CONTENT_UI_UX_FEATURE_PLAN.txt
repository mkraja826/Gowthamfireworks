1. PRODUCT EXPERIENCE PRINCIPLE
-------------------------------
Build one premium, mobile-first platform with three clearly separated experiences:

A. PUBLIC AND PERSONAL/B2C
- Celebration-focused landing page.
- Public retail catalogue.
- Personal customer phone OTP login.
- Retail cart/order-request flow.
- Account, saved addresses and request tracking.

B. BUSINESS/B2B
- Business profile and wholesale application.
- Owner approval before wholesale pricing is exposed.
- Wholesale catalogue with assigned price list, MOQ and carton quantities.
- Business quote/order-request history and application status.

C. OWNER/ADMIN
- Direct pre-authorised phone OTP login.
- Calm operational interface rather than festive public styling.
- Full control over products, images, prices, stock, customers, wholesale approvals, requests/orders and website content.

The public website should feel celebratory and premium. The admin area should prioritise speed, clarity and low-error operation.

2. THEME CONCEPT — “SIVAKASI MIDNIGHT FESTIVAL”
------------------------------------------------

Core idea:
- A dark midnight canvas makes fireworks and product packaging look vivid.
- Warm gold conveys factory heritage, quality and trust.
- Crimson and ember orange create energy and celebration.
- Product cards remain light or neutral so colourful boxes are easy to inspect.

The site must not look like:
- a casino;
- a gaming website;
- a crowded wholesale price poster;
- a generic black-and-gold luxury template;
- a children-only cartoon website;
- a payment-first e-commerce site.

Visual balance:
- 65% clean neutral space and product clarity.
- 25% midnight/navy atmosphere.
- 10% celebratory colour and glow.

3. PROPOSED DESIGN TOKENS
-------------------------

Primary colours:
- Midnight Navy: #070B1A — main dark background.
- Deep Indigo: #101735 — alternate dark surfaces.
- Festival Gold: #F5B942 — primary accent and premium details.
- Ember Orange: #FF6B2C — promotional accent and important highlights.
- Celebration Crimson: #C92A3D — offer badges and selective emphasis.
- Warm Ivory: #FFF8EC — warm light sections.
- Product White: #FFFFFF — catalogue cards and forms.
- Ink: #171923 — primary text on light backgrounds.
- Muted Slate: #667085 — secondary text.
- Success Green: #1F9D62 — available/approved/completed.
- Warning Amber: #D98C10 — limited/pending.
- Error Red: #C73737 — out of stock/rejected/destructive actions.

Rules:
- Gold is an accent, not body text.
- Red is not used for every button.
- Availability colours are consistent across public, account and admin screens.
- Product photos never sit directly on a noisy fireworks background.
- Every text/background pair must meet accessible contrast targets.

4. TYPOGRAPHY
-------------

Recommended direction:
- Display/headings: a strong, elegant serif or expressive Indian-display-compatible font used sparingly.
- Interface/body: a highly readable sans-serif.
- Numbers/prices/admin tables: tabular numerals where supported.

Practical font pairing candidates:
- Headings: DM Serif Display or Fraunces.
- Interface: Inter, Manrope or Plus Jakarta Sans.
- Tamil support can be added with Noto Sans Tamil when Tamil content is enabled.

Rules:
- No script fonts for long headings.
- Mobile body text minimum approximately 16 px.
- Price, quantity, MOQ and status must be easy to scan.

5. LOGO AND BRAND TREATMENT
---------------------------

Required logo variants:
- Full horizontal logo for desktop header.
- Compact mark for mobile header and favicon.
- White/light version for dark backgrounds.
- Dark version for invoices, print and light surfaces.

Brand motion:
- A short, subtle firework bloom may appear once in the hero or logo reveal.
- Avoid continuous flashing, rapid colour changes or loud autoplay effects.
- Respect reduced-motion user preferences.

6. IMAGE AND PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION
----------------------------------

Use real factory and product photography wherever possible:
- Product box front at a consistent angle.
- Optional side/back image for specifications.
- One clean primary product image on a neutral background.
- Factory exterior, packing process and team photographs for trust sections.
- Authentic warehouse/shop images only where legally and operationally suitable.

Product image rules:
- Prefer a square 1:1 primary image.
- Use a consistent crop and padding.
- Avoid watermarks from other sellers.
- Avoid low-resolution WhatsApp screenshots.
- Compress and convert to modern formats.
- Owner can set image focal point and primary image.

Decorative imagery:
- Wide night-sky fireworks footage or photography only in hero/background areas.
- Dark overlay behind text.
- Static fallback image for slow networks and reduced motion.

11. LOGIN AND TEST OTP EXPERIENCE
--------------------------------

Development mode:
- Use Supabase Test OTP numbers.
- Show a clear non-production banner only on local/staging environments.
- Never show the test OTP on production.

Customer route: /login
Step 1:
- Phone field with India +91 default and country selector if international use is retained.
- Continue button.
- Privacy/terms note.

Step 2:
- Six-digit OTP input.
- Auto-focus and paste support.
- Resend timer.
- Change phone number.
- Clear error/expiry state.

After verification:
- Existing profile is routed automatically.
- First-time customer sees two account-type cards only:
  Personal — buying for self/family.
  Business — shop, wholesaler or organisation.
- No Admin option.

Admin route: /admin/login
- Minimal branded screen.
- Phone and OTP.
- Pre-authorised role check.
- Direct /admin redirect for owner_admin.
- Non-admin users receive a safe access-denied message.

19. MOBILE UX RULES
-------------------

- Design mobile first, not desktop compressed later.
- Main action reachable with one thumb.
- Buttons at least approximately 44 px touch height.
- Sticky cart/add action where useful.
- Forms use correct keyboard types.
- OTP supports paste and one-time-code autofill.
- Avoid large blocking popups.
- Bottom sheets for filters and selectors.
- Preserve cart and partially completed business application.
- Show skeleton loading rather than blank screens.
- Compress images and lazy-load below the fold.
- WhatsApp and call actions are easy to reach but not intrusive.

20. ACCESSIBILITY, PERFORMANCE AND TRUST
---------------------------------------

Accessibility:
- Keyboard navigation.
- Visible focus states.
- Semantic headings and labels.
- Alt text for meaningful images.
- Reduced-motion mode.
- No essential information conveyed by colour alone.

Performance:
- Optimised images.
- Minimal hero video and static fallback.
- Server-rendered catalogue where appropriate.
- CDN caching for public assets.
- Avoid large animation libraries.
- Mobile slow-network testing.

Trust:
- Real business information.
- Current phone/WhatsApp details.
- Current price-list date.
- Clear request/confirmation wording.
- Accurate availability.
- Visible privacy and safety links.
- No fake countdown, fake stock scarcity or fake testimonials.

23. DESIGN ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
------------------------------

- The homepage clearly explains Personal/B2C and Business/B2B within the first screen and next section.
- A customer can find a product within a few taps.
- Product photography remains the strongest visual element in catalogue views.
- Personal and Business onboarding are clearly different.
- No public user can select Admin.
- Admin sees a direct, operational dashboard after authorised OTP login.
- Wholesale pricing is not rendered or returned to unapproved users.
- Retail and wholesale carts cannot mix.
- Every changing offer and business claim can be managed in admin.
- Mobile pages remain fast and usable on slower networks.
- The default request flow never implies automatic final sale or dispatch before owner confirmation.
