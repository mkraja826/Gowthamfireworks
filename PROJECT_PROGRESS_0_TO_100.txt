PROJECT GOAL
------------
Build one premium mobile-first platform serving:
- B2C: factory to personal/retail customer.
- B2B: factory to approved business/wholesaler.
- Owner/admin: catalogue, prices, photos, stock, applications, requests and website content without code editing.

CONFIRMED PRODUCT DIRECTION
---------------------------
Status: APPROVED

- Premium public landing page and searchable catalogue.
- Separate Personal/B2C and Business/B2B experiences.

DESIGN DIRECTION STATUS
-----------------------
Previous direction: “Sivakasi Midnight Festival”.
Current status: SUPERSEDED FOR PUBLIC UI AFTER SENIOR REVIEW.

Reason:
- The first implementation looked like a generic dark SaaS/startup template.
- It described platform architecture rather than helping customers shop.
- Emojis replaced real product imagery.
- Dark gradients, glass panels and Georgia headings did not create an authentic Gowtham identity.
- Public copy exposed development placeholders.
- The homepage lacked enough immediate commercial proof, contact and serviceability information.

New recommended direction: “Modern Sivakasi Market”.

Principles:
- 75–80% warm white/light product surfaces.
- Deep maroon/navy reserved for header, footer and selected banners.
- Saffron/gold used as controlled accents.
- Real product packaging provides most colour and energy.
- Real factory/team/warehouse imagery provides trust.
- Fast mobile catalogue, visible search, WhatsApp, cart and serviceability.
- Direct customer language instead of internal system language.

Detailed review:
- docs/UI_UX_SENIOR_REVIEW_AND_REDESIGN_PLAN.md
- Review commit: 8150b19c312ea88ad2568b4d090ebddff2c6f8c8

SENIOR UI/UX REVIEW — MAJOR FINDINGS
------------------------------------
Current source-level UI/UX maturity assessment: 4.5/10.

Critical public issues:
1. Hero panel shows internal architecture such as “2 buying paths”, “1 owner dashboard” and OTP instead of product/customer value.
2. Product and category imagery uses emojis rather than real crackers and packaging.
3. Public design is generic dark-template styling rather than a distinctive Sivakasi retail identity.
4. Inter is named but not loaded; Georgia is overused for headings and branding.
5. Public copy says the site is developing and that real information will be added later.
6. Homepage uses oversized repeated sections and abstract messaging before product/service proof.
7. Header lacks visible search and prominent WhatsApp support.
8. Public footer exposes Owner Login, contrary to the approved private-admin rule.
9. Catalogue has redundant category controls but lacks meaningful filter/sort tools.
10. Product cards lack real imagery and efficient mobile comparison.
11. Product detail lacks image gallery, sticky mobile CTA and stronger purchase support.
12. Cart lacks progress, serviceability and minimum-requirement guidance.
13. Trust is described but not proven with verified brands, contacts, factory images, service regions or current price-list information.
14. Accessibility needs focus-visible states, skip navigation, stronger mobile menu behaviour and improved semantics.

Critical admin issues:
1. Add Product is one long form.
2. Category creation uses window.prompt.
3. No image preview/crop/order workflow.
4. No autosave or unsaved-change warning.
5. No retail/wholesale card preview.
6. Product list lacks thumbnails, prices, category and edit actions.
7. Related product inserts are not transactional and can leave partial records.
